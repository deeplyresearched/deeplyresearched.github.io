Exam Deep Dive

PLAB 1: Applied Knowledge Test

  • ●Format: 180 single-best-answer MCQs.
  • ●Duration: 3 hours.
  • ●Content: Focuses on common, important, and acute conditions.

Sample 12-Week Study Plan for PLAB 1

Weeks 1-4: Core Foundations

  • Focus: Medicine (Cardio, Resp, Neuro)
  • Combine with basic Surgery topics
  • Practice 50-100 questions daily

Weeks 5-8: Specialties

  • Focus: Pediatrics, Obs & Gyn, Psychiatry
  • Continue with Surgery specialties
  • Start timed mock tests (half-length)

Weeks 9-12: Integration & Review

  • Focus: Ethics, Other minor topics
  • Revise all subjects, focus on weak areas
  • Complete full-length mock exams weekly

Doctor's Life in the UK

An overview of pay, working conditions, and work-life balance for doctors in the NHS.

Understanding Your Pay (Payscale)

Your total salary is more than just a basic figure. It's composed of several elements:

  • ●
    Basic Pay: Determined by your training stage (e.g., FY1, ST3). This increases annually.
  • ●
    Banding / Pay Enhancement: A significant supplement (often 30-50% of basic pay) for working hours outside of 9am-5pm Monday-Friday. This includes nights and weekends.
  • ●
    On-Call Allowance: An additional payment for being available to work, even if not called in.

For the most accurate and detailed pay scales, refer to the official NHS Employers website.

Living Conditions

Costs vary significantly by location. Major cities like London are expensive, while cities in the North of England (e.g., Manchester, Leeds) offer a lower cost of living. Many junior doctors share flats or houses to manage costs. Hospital accommodation may be available but is often limited.

Work-Life Balance & Working Hours

The NHS is a demanding environment, but protections are in place. The European Working Time Directive (EWTD), which the UK still largely adheres to, limits the working week to an average of 48 hours. However, this is an average, and some weeks will be more intense than others.

  • Rotas are typically planned months in advance, allowing you to plan your personal life.
  • You are entitled to annual leave, study leave for professional development, and sick pay.
  • The balance can be challenging, especially during busy rotations in specialties like A&E or acute medicine, but it is a key focus for the GMC and NHS Trusts.

UK Career Progression

Passing PLAB is the start of your NHS career. Understand the typical progression path from junior doctor to a senior role.

Stage 1: Foundation Programme (FY1 & FY2)

A two-year programme with rotations through various specialties to build a broad base of experience. Full GMC registration is granted after successfully completing FY1.

Stage 2: Core / Specialty Training (CT/ST)

After the Foundation Programme, you apply for training in a specific area (e.g., Medicine, Surgery, GP). This lasts 3-8 years depending on the specialty.

Stage 3: CCT & Senior Roles

Upon completion of training, you receive your CCT, allowing you to apply for senior posts as a Consultant or a General Practitioner.
