Reshaping Global Finance

A core ambition of the BRICS+ bloc is to create a multipolar financial world, reducing dependency on the US dollar and Western-led institutions like the IMF and World Bank. This is primarily being pursued through the New Development Bank (NDB) and promoting trade in local currencies.

NDB Project Funding Growth

1. Alternative to IMF/World Bank

The New Development Bank (NDB) offers infrastructure and sustainable development loans with a focus on emerging economies, providing an alternative source of funding without the policy conditionalities often attached to Western loans.

2. De-Dollarization Initiatives

Members are increasingly settling bilateral trade in local currencies (e.g., yuan, rupee, ruble), bypassing the SWIFT system and reducing exposure to US sanctions and monetary policy.

Shifting Power Dynamics

The rise of BRICS+ presents a direct challenge to the post-WWII global order long dominated by the G7. This shift is most visible in the structure of global governance institutions, where BRICS+ advocates for a more equitable distribution of power.
