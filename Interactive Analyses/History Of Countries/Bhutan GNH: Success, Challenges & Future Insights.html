A Holistic Approach to Development

Gross National Happiness (GNH) is a philosophy that guides the government of Bhutan, emphasizing collective happiness and well-being as the primary goal of development. It challenges the conventional wisdom that economic growth is the ultimate objective, proposing instead a balanced approach that integrates material well-being with spiritual, cultural, and environmental health. This interactive report explores the framework, impact, and challenges of Bhutan's unique vision.

Navigating the Challenges Ahead

Despite its successes, Bhutan faces significant challenges in implementing the GNH framework in a rapidly globalizing world. Balancing tradition with modernity remains a constant endeavor. Explore some of the key challenges below.

Youth Unemployment ▾

A growing youth population faces a shortage of suitable jobs, creating a mismatch between the aspirations of educated young people and the opportunities available in the economy. This poses a risk to social stability and individual well-being.

Economic Vulnerability ▾

Bhutan's economy is heavily reliant on hydropower exports to India and foreign aid. This lack of diversification makes it vulnerable to external shocks and climate change impacts on glacial water sources.

Cultural Preservation vs. Globalization ▾

The influx of global media and technology presents both opportunities and challenges. While connecting Bhutan to the world, it also exerts pressure on traditional values, languages, and cultural practices, which are central to the GNH philosophy.

Ensuring Equity ▾

While poverty has decreased, disparities in income and access to services persist between urban and rural areas. Ensuring that the benefits of development are shared equitably across all segments of society is a continuing priority.
