An interactive analysis of the Israel-Hamas war's ripple effects across the Middle East.

The Widening Conflict

The conflict, which began with Hamas's attack on October 7, 2023, has rapidly evolved from a localized war into a multi-front regional crisis. Its effects are not contained within Gaza's borders but are sending shockwaves across the Middle East, impacting economies, societies, and international security dynamics.

This interactive report provides a structured overview of these cascading effects. Explore the different sections to understand the specific economic, humanitarian, and security implications for neighboring countries and the broader international community. The crisis has drawn in regional powers and non-state actors, creating a volatile web of conflicts from the Levant to the Red Sea.

Economic Fallout

The conflict has triggered severe economic shocks, disrupting key sectors like tourism and global trade while straining the already fragile economies of neighboring states.

Neighboring Economies Under Strain

GDP growth forecasts have been significantly revised downwards for countries reliant on tourism and regional stability.

Red Sea Shipping Disruption

Houthi attacks on commercial vessels have forced major shipping lines to reroute, causing a sharp decline in traffic through the vital Suez Canal.

International Response

Global and regional powers have responded with a mix of diplomacy, military action, and humanitarian aid, reflecting complex geopolitical interests.

Actor Primary Stance & Actions Key Role
United States Strong military and diplomatic support for Israel. Leads Operation Prosperity Guardian in the Red Sea to counter Houthi attacks. Pushes for ceasefires and humanitarian aid delivery. Primary Ally / Military Actor
European Union Politically divided but generally condemns Hamas, supports a two-state solution, and provides significant humanitarian aid. Participates in maritime security missions. Diplomatic & Humanitarian Actor
Egypt & Qatar Serve as the primary mediators in ceasefire and hostage negotiations between Israel and Hamas. Egypt manages the critical Rafah border crossing for aid. Key Mediators
Jordan Faces significant internal pressure due to its large Palestinian population. Engages in diplomacy and facilitates aid delivery, while managing domestic stability. Frontline State / Stabilizer
Russia & China Generally critical of the US role and Western response. Use diplomatic platforms like the UN to call for immediate ceasefires and position themselves as alternative global powers. Geopolitical Counterweights
