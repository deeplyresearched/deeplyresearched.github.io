Core Concepts & Technologies

Expand the sections below to learn about the foundational principles of PC building.

In the pursuit of peak gaming performance, a "bottleneck" is an inherent and unavoidable characteristic of any computer system. The art of building a balanced gaming PC is not about eliminating bottlenecks, but about strategically designing the system so the primary limitation aligns with the intended use case. For gaming, the ideal state is to be "GPU-bound," where the graphics card is consistently at or near 100% utilization. This ensures the most expensive component delivers its maximum possible value.

The performance burden shifts based on resolution. At 1080p, the CPU's role is more pronounced, as it must prepare a high volume of frames for the GPU. At 1440p and 4K, the GPU load increases dramatically, making it the primary limiting factor. A truly balanced system requires careful consideration of not just the hardware, but the specific games and resolutions it is intended to run.

The 2025 hardware landscape is defined by fierce competition between NVIDIA, AMD, and Intel, with each introducing new architectures that fundamentally change how frames are rendered. Understanding these is essential to making an informed investment.

  • NVIDIA's Blackwell & DLSS 4: Introduces 4th-gen RT Cores and 5th-gen Tensor Cores. Its flagship feature, DLSS 4 with Multi-Frame Generation (MFG), uses a transformer AI model to generate up to three new frames for every one rendered, creating a closed ecosystem that drives sales of premium hardware.
  • AMD's Zen 5 & RDNA 4: The Zen 5 CPU architecture delivers a ~16% IPC uplift with dramatic power efficiency gains. On the graphics front, RDNA 4 with FSR 4 moves to a hardware-locked, machine-learning pipeline, improving quality but restricting it to the latest AMD GPUs.
  • Intel's Arrow Lake & Battlemage: Arrow Lake CPUs use a tile-based architecture for power efficiency gains. Battlemage GPUs are paired with XeSS 2, which includes AI frame generation. Intel's key strategy is making its XeSS SDK open-source, allowing it to work on competing hardware to encourage widespread developer adoption.
