Beginner's Step-by-Step Account Creation Guide

Follow this simplified guide to successfully create your AMC account and prepare your credentials for examination authorization.

Phase 1: AMC Account & EPIC Verification

  1. Create AMC Account: Register on the AMC website. This forms your unique candidate number and portfolio.
  2. Pay Portfolio Fee: A non-refundable fee is required to activate your portfolio and proceed to verification.
  3. EPIC Application: Use the AMC portal to initiate Primary Source Verification (PSV) through EPIC. You must upload documents like your medical diploma and transcript.
  4. Wait for PSV: Allow several weeks for EPIC to verify your medical degree directly with your issuing institution.

Phase 2: Examination Authorization

  1. EPIC Completion: Once EPIC confirms your degree is genuine, the AMC is notified.
  2. Apply for MCQ Exam: Return to your AMC portfolio to formally apply for the CAT MCQ Exam. Pay the substantial exam application fee.
  3. Receive Authorization: The AMC will issue an Authorization Letter (valid for 12 months) via your portal.
  4. Book Exam Date: Use the details in the authorization letter to book your specific exam slot with the testing provider (Pearson VUE) at your preferred location globally.

Key Concepts & Terminology

Understanding the core terminology of the Australian medical system is vital for IMGs navigating the registration process. This section defines key pathways and terms.

EPIC (ECFMG)

The Electronic Portfolio of International Credentials, used for Primary Source Verification of your medical degree. This is a mandatory first step before applying for the AMC exams.

Standard Pathway

The most common route for IMGs, requiring successful completion of the AMC CAT MCQ and Clinical Examinations, followed by supervised practice.

Competent Authority Pathway

A streamlined route for IMGs from specific recognized countries (e.g., UK, Canada, USA, Ireland, New Zealand) that allows for a shorter pathway to general registration without the AMC exams.

Specialist Pathway

For IMGs who have completed specialist training overseas and wish to have their qualifications assessed for substantial equivalence to an Australian Fellowship (FRACGP, FRACP, etc.).

Supervised Practice

A mandatory 12-month period of supervised work as a medical practitioner in an Australian hospital or practice, required after passing the AMC exams to gain full general registration.

A Deep Dive into the AMC Exams

The AMC assessment is a two-part process designed to test your medical knowledge and clinical skills against the standard for Australian medical graduates. View the details and see static practice examples below.

CAT MCQ Examination Details

This is a computer-adaptive test that assesses your fundamental medical knowledge. The difficulty of questions adjusts based on your answers, providing a precise evaluation of your abilities. Authorization is valid for 12 months.

⏱
Duration: 3.5 hours (210 minutes)
?
Questions: 150 MCQs (120 scored, 30 pilot)
💻
Format: Computer Adaptive Test (CAT) - must answer all 120 scored items for a result.

Sample Practice Question

A 65-year-old male with a history of hypertension presents with acute-onset, severe, tearing chest pain radiating to his back. His blood pressure is 180/110 mmHg and heart rate is 105 bpm. What is the most likely diagnosis?

A. Acute Myocardial Infarction
B. Aortic Dissection
C. Pulmonary Embolism
D. Pericarditis
Rationale: The classic presentation of sudden, tearing pain radiating to the back in a hypertensive patient strongly suggests Aortic Dissection (B).

Clinical Examination (OSCE) Details

This is a practical, multi-station examination assessing clinical skills in realistic scenarios. You must pass 9 out of 14 scored stations to clear the exam.

👥
Stations: 20 total (16 assessed, 4 rest/transition)
⏱
Timing: 10 minutes per station (2 min reading, 8 min assessment)
✓
Passing Score: Pass 9 out of 14 scored stations.

Sample Clinical Scenario

Scenario: You are the resident in the Emergency Department. Mrs. Smith, a 78-year-old with a known history of atrial fibrillation, is brought in with acute breathlessness. She is visibly distressed.

Tasks:
  1. Take a focused history relevant to her acute breathlessness.
  2. Perform a focused respiratory and cardiovascular examination.
  3. Explain your differential diagnoses and immediate management plan to the patient.

Sample Ethical Dilemma

A 16-year-old patient who is a Jehovah's Witness requires a life-saving blood transfusion, but refuses treatment. Their parents agree with the patient's decision. What is your immediate course of action?

Career, Compensation, and Lifestyle in Australia

Australia offers competitive pay, generous entitlements, and a high quality of life. Understanding the financial and cultural context is key to a successful transition.

1. Detailed Pay Scale (Base Salary)

Salaries are dictated by state and territory industrial agreements, meaning pay is consistent across public hospitals in a state, and often higher with penalty rates for nights/weekends.

Position AUD (Approx.)
Intern / RMO (PGY1-3) $75,000 - $110,000
Registrar (Trainee) $120,000 - $180,000+
Consultant $200,000 - $450,000+

2. Work-Life Balance and Entitlements

Australia's medical system prioritizes physician well-being. This is enshrined in mandatory agreements that protect working hours and ensure generous paid leave.

  • ✓
    Annual Leave: Typically 4 to 6 weeks paid annual leave per year, plus access to local public holidays.
  • ✓
    Fatigue Management: Strict limits on consecutive shifts and maximum working hours per roster cycle, with mandated rest periods between shifts.
  • ✓
    Professional Development: Access to paid professional development leave and funding to support ongoing education and training.

3. Living Conditions and Quality of Life

Australia consistently ranks high globally for quality of life, benefiting from robust public services and a strong economy.

  • ★
    Housing Costs: Highly varied. Metropolitan centers (Sydney/Melbourne) are among the most expensive globally; regional areas offer significantly lower costs and higher job incentives.
  • ★
    Healthcare Access: Excellent access to Australia's universal healthcare system (Medicare) and robust private options.
  • ★
    Culture & Environment: Multicultural, safe society with easy access to unique natural environments (beaches, mountains, outback) supporting an active lifestyle.

Post-Exam: The Road to General Registration

Passing the AMC exams grants you the AMC certificate, but the final step is achieving general registration with the Medical Board of Australia (MBA). This requires finding and completing an accredited period of supervised practice.

1

Apply for Provisional Registration

After receiving your AMC Certificate, you must apply to the MBA for Provisional Registration, which is necessary to work in an Australian medical position.

2

Secure a Supervised Position

Find an accredited intern or Resident Medical Officer (RMO) position, often through state health recruitment systems (e.g., NSW Health, Victorian Health). This is a competitive stage.

3

Complete 12 Months of Supervised Practice

Work under supervision, typically rotating through multiple terms (e.g., Medicine, Surgery, Emergency). Your supervisor must periodically assess and report on your performance.

4

Achieve General Registration

Once the MBA receives satisfactory reports on your supervised practice, you are eligible to apply for General Registration, allowing you to practice independently in Australia.

Study Resources and Sample Planning

Effective planning is crucial for success. Here are general study timelines and recommended official resources to guide your preparation for the AMC CAT MCQ exam.

Official Recommended Resources

  • AMC Handbook of Clinical Assessment.
  • AMC practice exams and official multiple-choice questions sets.
  • The Australian Therapeutic Guidelines (Therapeutic Guidelines eTG).
  • Relevant Australian Public Health guidelines and legislation.
  • Online question banks (Q-banks) specific to the AMC curriculum.

Sample 6-Month Study Plan (MCQ)

Months 1-2 (Foundation): Review core concepts (Adult Health, Child Health). Focus on high-yield topics and system-based revision.
Months 3-4 (Integration): Focus on Women's Health, Mental Health, Population Health, and Ethics. Integrate knowledge with question practice.
Months 5-6 (Simulation): Dedicated mock exam period. Take weekly full-length practice tests under timed conditions. Focus on reviewing wrong answers and identifying weak areas.
