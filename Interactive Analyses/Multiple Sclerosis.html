What is Multiple Sclerosis?

MS is a long-term condition affecting the brain and spinal cord. It's an autoimmune disease where the body's own defense system mistakenly attacks the protective sheath covering nerve fibers.

An Electrical Wire Analogy

Think of your nerves as electrical wires. These wires are covered in a protective insulation called **myelin**. In MS, the immune system damages this myelin, disrupting the flow of electrical signals from your brain to your body. This disruption causes the wide range of MS symptoms.

The Diagnostic Pathway

Diagnosis relies on the 2017 McDonald Criteria, which require showing that lesions have occurred in different parts of the CNS (**Dissemination in Space - DIS**) and at different times (**Dissemination in Time - DIT**).

Disease-Modifying Therapies (DMTs)

DMTs are the cornerstone of long-term care, aiming to reduce relapses and slow disability progression. They are broadly categorized by efficacy.

Symptomatic Management

While DMTs treat the underlying disease, a wide range of therapies are used to manage the daily symptoms of MS and improve quality of life.

Non-Pharmacological

  • Physiotherapy: For mobility, strength, and balance.
  • Occupational Therapy: For daily living strategies and energy conservation.
  • Lifestyle: Regular exercise, a balanced diet, and stress management.

Pharmacological

  • Spasticity: Medications like Baclofen and Tizanidine.
  • Neuropathic Pain: Agents like Gabapentin and Amitriptyline.
  • Fatigue: Stimulants such as Amantadine or Modafinil.
  • Bladder Issues: Anticholinergics or beta-3 agonists.
