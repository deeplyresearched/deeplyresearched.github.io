Your Complete Guide to the DHA License for IMGs

The Dubai Health Authority (DHA) exam is the gateway for International Medical Graduates (IMGs) and other healthcare professionals to practice in Dubai. This guide breaks down the entire process into clear, manageable sections to help you navigate your journey with confidence.

Eligibility & Pathway

Understanding the requirements is the first and most critical step. DHA has specific criteria regarding education and experience.

Core Eligibility Requirements

  • Educational Qualifications: Your degree must be from a medical school/university listed in the World Directory of Medical Schools (WDOMS).
  • Professional Experience: For General Practitioners (GPs), a minimum of two years of clinical experience post-internship is required. For specialists, this is typically three years or more in your specialty after your master's degree.
  • Licensure: You must hold a valid and current professional license to practice in your home country or country of last employment.
  • Good Standing: A Good Standing Certificate (GSC) from the medical council/authority you are registered with is mandatory. It must be recent (usually issued within the last 6 months).

Exams & Preparation

The DHA assessment is designed to test your clinical knowledge and decision-making skills according to UAE healthcare standards.

Exam Format

For most professions, the assessment is a Computer-Based Test (CBT). Key features include:

  • Structure: Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs).
  • Duration: Typically 2-3 hours.
  • Content: Covers topics relevant to your specialty. Focuses on clinical scenarios.
  • Administration: Conducted globally at Prometric test centers.

Syllabus & Resources

DHA does not provide a fixed syllabus. The exam is based on your specialty's core competencies. Recommended resources include:

  • Standard international textbooks for your specialty.
  • Review materials for board exams (e.g., USMLE, PLAB, MRCP).
  • Online question banks and mock exams specific to Gulf country medical exams.

Sample 3-Month Study Plan

A structured approach to your preparation can significantly increase your chances of success. Here is a suggested timeline.

Month 1: Foundation

Review core subjects and fundamental concepts. Identify weak areas. Go through major clinical guidelines relevant to your field.

Month 2: Application

Focus on solving MCQs and clinical scenarios. Start with topic-wise questions and gradually move to mixed-subject tests.

Month 3: Simulation

Take full-length mock exams under timed conditions. Analyze your performance, review mistakes, and work on time management.

Salary & Lifestyle in Dubai

Dubai offers competitive, tax-free salaries and a high standard of living for healthcare professionals.

Illustrative Monthly Salaries (AED)

Salaries vary based on experience, specialty, and the employing facility (government vs. private).

  • General Practitioner: 18,000 - 30,000+
  • Specialist Physician: 35,000 - 60,000+
  • Registered Nurse: 7,000 - 14,000+
  • Pharmacist: 10,000 - 18,000+

Lifestyle Benefits

  • Tax-Free Income: Your salary is not subject to income tax.
  • Multicultural Environment: Live and work with people from all over the world.
  • Safety & Security: Dubai is one of the safest cities globally.
  • Travel Hub: Excellent connectivity for international travel.
  • Benefits Package: Employers typically offer health insurance, annual air tickets, and paid leave.
