Core Strategic Vision

The Volkswagen Group's "NEW AUTO" strategy aims to transform the company from a traditional vehicle manufacturer into a leading, software-driven mobility provider. This vision is built upon a foundation of strong brands, global scale, and cutting-edge technology platforms.

Key pillars include a unified technology platform for electric vehicles (SSP), in-house battery production via PowerCo, and an integrated mobility services ecosystem. The goal is to create a seamless, sustainable, and safe mobility experience for customers, generating new revenue pools and ensuring long-term competitiveness in a rapidly evolving industry.

Core Technology Platforms

A key element of VW's strategy is the use of scalable, shared platforms across its brands. This approach reduces complexity, lowers costs, and accelerates development, allowing for tailored vehicles on a common technological foundation.

MEB

Modular Electric Drive Matrix

The foundational platform for the Group's volume electric models. It's highly flexible, supporting various body styles from compact cars to vans.

Used by: VW ID. Series, Audi Q4 e-tron, ≈†koda Enyaq, Cupra Born.

PPE

Premium Platform Electric

Co-developed by Audi and Porsche, this 800-volt architecture is designed for premium and luxury electric vehicles, enabling high performance and fast charging.

Used by: Porsche Macan EV, Audi A6 e-tron.

SSP

Scalable Systems Platform

The future unified platform. SSP will eventually replace all others, designed to be all-electric, fully digital, and highly scalable for all models from all brands.

Future platform for all Group brands post-2026.

SWOT Analysis

A summary of the Volkswagen Group's internal strengths and weaknesses, alongside external opportunities and threats in the current market.

Strengths

  • Diverse and strong brand portfolio covering all segments.
  • Massive global manufacturing footprint and scale.
  • Significant investment capacity for R&D and electrification.
  • Strong market position in Europe and China.

Weaknesses

  • Complexity in managing numerous brands and platforms.
  • Software development (via CARIAD) has faced delays and challenges.
  • High cost structure compared to newer EV-only competitors.
  • Lower market penetration in North America.

Opportunities

  • Growth in the global EV market.
  • New revenue streams from software, mobility services, and autonomous driving.
  • Leveraging PowerCo for battery production and supply chain control.
  • Growth potential in emerging markets with tailored products.

Threats

  • Intense competition from Tesla, BYD, and other Chinese EV makers.
  • Geopolitical tensions affecting supply chains and market access.
  • Potential for slower-than-expected EV adoption in some regions.
  • Regulatory changes regarding emissions and trade policies.
