The Heart of the Beast

At the core of the ZR1 lies a groundbreaking powertrain designed for maximum output and response. Here, we explore the two key systems that deliver its earth-shattering performance. Click the tabs below to learn more about the bespoke engine and the advanced drivetrain that puts the power to the pavement.

LT7 5.5L Twin-Turbo V8

The centerpiece of the ZR1 is the rumored LT7 engine. Building on the Z06's high-revving, flat-plane crank LT6, the LT7 adds two turbochargers to dramatically increase power and torque across the entire rev range. This bespoke engine is expected to produce around 850 horsepower and 825 lb-ft of torque, making it the most powerful production engine ever fitted to a Corvette. The engineering focus is on minimizing turbo lag and delivering a linear, explosive powerband suitable for both road and track domination.

Advanced All-Wheel Drive

To effectively harness the LT7's immense power, the ZR1 is widely expected to feature an advanced all-wheel-drive system. Drawing inspiration from the Corvette E-Ray's electrified front axle, the ZR1's system will likely be tailored for pure performance, possibly without the hybrid components. This would provide incredible launch traction for sub-2.5 second 0-60 times and enhance stability and cornering grip, allowing the driver to exploit the car's full potential in all conditions. The system will be paired with the C8's quick-shifting 8-speed dual-clutch transaxle, recalibrated for the ZR1's extreme output.

Sculpted for Speed

The ZR1's form is dictated by function. Every scoop, wing, and surface is designed to manipulate air for maximum downforce and cooling. This section breaks down the key aerodynamic components that keep the ZR1 glued to the track at extreme speeds. Click on each feature to understand its critical role.

Front Splitter & Dive Planes

The massive front splitter extends far forward to generate significant front-end downforce, preventing lift at high speeds and ensuring sharp turn-in. It will be complemented by dive planes on the corners of the bumper to further direct air and create stabilizing vortices.

Active Rear Wing

Expected to be the centerpiece of the aero package, a large, active rear wing will adjust its angle of attack in real-time. It will flatten on straights to reduce drag for higher top speeds (like a DRS system) and increase its angle under braking and in corners for maximum downforce and stability.

Widebody & Functional Venting

The ZR1 will feature significantly wider fenders to house larger, stickier tires. The body will be covered in functional vents, including large side intakes to feed the turbochargers' intercoolers and fender vents to extract high-pressure air from the wheel wells, reducing lift and improving cooling.

Full Underbody Tray & Diffuser

A flat underbody will smooth airflow beneath the car, creating a low-pressure zone that effectively sucks the car to the road. This effect is amplified at the rear by a massive, aggressive diffuser that helps extract the air quickly, generating a huge portion of the car's total downforce without adding significant drag.
