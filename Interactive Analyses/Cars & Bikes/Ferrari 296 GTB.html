Aerodynamics & Chassis Dynamics

The 296 GTB's performance is not just about power; it's about control. A suite of advanced electronic systems and active aerodynamics ensures the car is both incredibly fast and surprisingly agile.

Active Rear Spoiler

Inspired by the LaFerrari, an active aero device generates high rear downforce. In high-downforce conditions, it delivers an extra 100 kg of downforce at 250 km/h.

'Tea-Tray' Front Aero

The front bumper design integrates a 'tea-tray' that directs hot air from the radiator away from the underbody, increasing aerodynamic efficiency and downforce over the front axle.

ABS 'evo' Controller

A world-first in the automotive sector. It uses data from the 6-way Chassis Dynamic Sensor (6w-CDS) for more precise braking force estimation, allowing for shorter stopping distances and better repeatability.

Brake-By-Wire

The brake-by-wire system allows for variable brake pedal feel and manages regenerative braking, seamlessly blending it with the hydraulic system for consistent performance under all conditions.

Short Wheelbase

The 296 GTB has a wheelbase 50mm shorter than previous Ferrari mid-engined berlinettas. This significantly enhances the car's agility and responsiveness, making it feel incredibly nimble.

Side Dynamics Control (FDE2.0)

Ferrari's advanced stability control system works with the electronic differential (E-Diff) and other sensors to provide maximum performance when cornering and exiting corners, especially in high-performance driving modes.

Modern Form, Classic Inspiration

The 296 GTB's design is a testament to modern aerodynamics while paying homage to iconic Ferraris of the past, particularly the 1963 250 LM. Its compact, clean lines define a new era of Ferrari berlinettas.

Key Design Elements

  • Visor-Effect Cockpit: The windscreen wraps around onto the side windows for a seamless, modern visor look, emphasizing the cabin's architecture.
  • 250 LM-Inspired B-Pillar: The break in the B-pillar is a clear reference to classic models like the 250 LM, merging heritage with a modern aesthetic.
  • Vertical Rear Screen: A unique vertical rear screen sits between two flying buttresses, showcasing the engine bay in an unconventional and striking way.
  • Kamm Tail: The rear of the car features a Kamm tail design, an aerodynamic principle that reduces drag by abruptly cutting off the tail, which also adds to its muscular stance.
  • Single Central Exhaust: A single, large exhaust pipe is integrated centrally into the lower part of the bumper, giving the rear a clean, aggressive, and contemporary look.
250 LM

The muscular rear haunches, B-pillar design, and overall compact form of the 296 GTB are a modern interpretation of the legendary 1963 Ferrari 250 LM.
