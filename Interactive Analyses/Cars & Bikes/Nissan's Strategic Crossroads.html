The "Ambition 2030" Plan

This is Nissan's long-term vision for empowering mobility and beyond. The plan centers on accelerating electrification, advancing autonomous technologies, and fostering a sustainable ecosystem. Here we explore the core components of this strategy, from investment allocation to key technological milestones.

Phase 1: Foundation (2022-2024)

Focus on launching key EV models like the Ariya, strengthening the e-POWER hybrid lineup, and initiating pilot programs for autonomous services. This phase establishes the manufacturing and technology base for future growth.

Phase 2: Acceleration (2025-2027)

Ramp up production of new EVs and expand battery manufacturing capacity globally. Begin deployment of next-generation ProPILOT autonomous driving systems and prepare for the introduction of solid-state batteries.

Phase 3: Realization (2028-2030)

Launch vehicles with all-solid-state batteries (ASSB), aiming for significant cost reductions. Expand vehicle intelligence and connected services, fully integrating mobility solutions into smart ecosystems.

Global Perspective

Nissan faces a complex global landscape. In Asia, particularly China, competition from domestic EV brands is fierce. Europe's stringent emission regulations are accelerating the EV transition, creating both an opportunity and a compliance challenge. In North America, the market is rapidly growing, but consumer preference for larger vehicles like trucks and SUVs requires a tailored electrification strategy.
